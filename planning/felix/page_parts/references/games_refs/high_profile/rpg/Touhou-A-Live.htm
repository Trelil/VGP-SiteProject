Novelization

No, really. Someone was writing a novelization of Touhou-A-Live.
He admits it's kind of silly to write a fanfic about an (unfinished) fangame. That doesn't help much.
Here are some excerpts so you know what you're missing.

CAN'T SPOIL THE GREATEST LITERARY ACHIEVEMENT OF THE CENTURY

Assuming knowledge of a certain game from about ten years ago by, er... Circlesoft, and knowledge of Touhou Project, it's a rather interesting game.
Now, since it's a fangame, if you don't know the aforementioned game, well... it might be a little different.
Ah, well, what do I care?
Interesting or not? That's for the player to decide.
At the very least, the author thought "Yahoo! This is amazing!" as he bashed his fingers against the keyboard.

- - - - -

My name is Hong Meiling. Get it right, it's Hong Mei-ling.
You may hear people saying "China" or "Hon Meirin," but I don't approve of that kind of nonsense.

- - - - -

Youkai live longer than humans. That gives them a lot more time to waste.
Personally, I spend most of my free time sleeping.

- - - - -

"Your lose record could get you into Guiness!"
Guiness. The Guiness Book. A book from the outside world that recorded "world's firsts" - I'd heard of it from Patchouli.

- - - - -

"You are weak. And so long as you are weak, I want you to become strong."
I'm weak... not strong.
I don't even compare to Mistress or Miss Sakuya.
I'm... weak.

- - - - -

I heard a voice. A refreshing, relieving voice.
I couldn't understand the voice, but he was clearly talking to me.
"Hello, world!"
H... Haloo... warudo?
"Yes, good job! I'm impressed! You even put it into kana! The one who built you must've been pretty good."

- - - - -

"Living things have three kinds of memories. Through study, they learn "brain memories." Through training, they learn "body memories." And... there's one more. "Heart memories.""
...Heart?
"Yes, heart. People live with their hearts. They stand, walk, fall, cry, grow, raise, and trust... people that do that have lived with their hearts."
But... we aren't people.
We're systems. Machines. Not living things.
"But... there's no rule that says machines can't have hearts. Where are you going to find a rulebook that says machines can't stand, walk, fall, cry, grow, raise, and trust?"
...No rule.

- - - - -

"Hello, world!"

Hello, world.
Hello, everyone.
Hello, me.
But... that's all I really understand.

- - - - -

...One new message received.

To my child, who still doesn't know her name.
There are so many things I should apologize to you for, but I can't.
For making your life one that comes to nothing, I have been an awful girl.
With the Magicom, I was able to put a spirit into a mechanical body, but in the end, its life would mean nothing and it would be abused as a machine.
It was... egotistical of me.
Yet, I couldn't stop creating you.
Even if your life came to mean nothing, I wanted to have you.
Deciding that you should not be burdened with sin to atone for, I made you.
So, at the very least, I would see your happiness.
If... if you can, please forgive me.
And we'll all play, together.

To my beloved child. ...Alice Margatroid.

I understood. ...I finally understood.
It was what she had wanted to tell me. What Alice wanted to tell me.
I couldn't explain it, but I understood.

This was where the heart was.

Machines, humans, youkai... in name alone, they have no relation.
But when their heads are stroked, they are happy.
When they can all be together, they have fun.
And when they are all dead, they are sad.
Inside of me... inside of anyone, there is a heart.
